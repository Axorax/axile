Rules of Writing Chemical Equations
03 September 2022 •
What is a Chemical Equation?
A chemical equation is the representation of a chemical reaction using symbols of the elements to indicate the amount of substance, usually in moles, of each reactant and product. In short, a chemical equation is a shortened expression of a chemical reaction.
Rules of writing a chemical equation
To write a chemical equation, a set of some specific rules needs to be followed. They are given below:
  1. The formulas or symbols of the reacting substances or reactants are written on the left side of the sign of equality(=) and the formulas or symbols of the products are written on the right.

  2. If the reactants or products are more than one, a plus(+) sign is put between the formulas or symbols.

  3. If the number of molecule is more than one then the number is used before the formula.

  4. In between the reactants and products, an arrow sign(→) is used to show the way the reaction is occuring. Instead of an arrow sign, an equal sign(=) can also be used but both sides of the equation must be balanced.

  5. The number of atoms of different elements present in the reactants before chemical reaction must be equal to the number of atoms of different elements in the products formed after the reaction. In order to make balance, the symbol or formula must be multiplied by the required number.
Examples of chemical equation
H2 + O2  →  H2O
6CO2 + 6H2O  →  C6H12O6 + 6O2
Fe + S  →  FeS
