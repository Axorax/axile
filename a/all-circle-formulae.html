What is a circle?
A circle is a two-dimensional figure that can be described entirely in terms of its radius and diameter. The points at which the line segment that extends from any point on the circumference to the center divides the circumference into two identical halves are called diameters of the circle.
What are all the formulas for a circle?
A circle mainly has three formulas. They can be used for various purposes like finding the diameter, area, etc. Here is a figure of a circle:
Let us consider,

o = Center of the circle
c = Circumference
d = Diameter
r = Radius
a = Area of a circle


All the formulas for a circle are given in the table below:

Diameter of a circle d = 2 × r
Circumference of a circle c = 2 × π × r
or, c = 2πr
Area of a circle a = π × r²
or, a = πr²
Solving questions by using the above formulas
Given,

Radius, r = 9m

We know,

Diameter = 2r
= 2 × 9 m
= 18 m

Area = πr²
= 3.14 × 9² m²
= 254.34 m²

Circumference = 2πr
= 2 × 3.14 × 9 m
= 56.52 m (Ans.)
